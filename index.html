Linguistics

My interest in languages started when I was in middle school. I was attempting to learn Russian by my self at the time. Even though I obviously failed miserably at actually learning Russian, I did learn a great deal about linguistics in general and how languages work. My dad would turn the wifi off at night to make sure I went to bed so I would download pdfs of linguistics textbooks off the internet and read them. My favourite field of linguistics is historical linguistics which pertains to how languages change over time.

Reading

As a child, I hated reading. Watching TV was so much more interesting. Nonetheless, my parents would force me to read because they felt like it was an important skill for a child of my age to be able to have. When they started teaching Greek mythology in grade six, I fell head over heels with the subject and I immediately began reading as many books as I could about it. My parents were baffled when I refused to go to bed one day because I was engrossed in a book about the War of Troy. Those books had a lot of big words in them so they not only intiated my love of reading, but also expanded my vocabulary a great deal.
