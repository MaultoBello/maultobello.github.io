Global poverty decreased by a half between 2000 and 2015, but over 700 million people still live on less than $1.90 USD a day. Many of those that are most effected come from developing countries in Africa and Asia and lack the means to access the resources that could help them.

Economic growth and increased agricultural productivity has significantly reduced the amount of people who suffer from malnutrition. However, one out of every four Africans still go hungry and over 90 million children under the age of five are classified as being dangerously underweight.

On average, people today live much healthier lives than those of previous decades. Nonetheless, many around the world are still dying prematurely from preventable diseases. Poor health prevents individuals from completing their schooling and securing stable employment thus plunging them deeper into destitution.

Proper education is a right that many in developed countries take for granted. More than half of the adolescents around the world do not meet the minimum standards in subjects like reading and mathematics. Teachers and educators must invest in proper planning and infrastructure to improve the quality of education for their students.
